Into the Unknown

You stay low, moving with painstaking precision. The creature’s ragged breathing rattles through the corridor behind you, its claws skittering lightly against the metal floor. It hasn’t given up—it’s still searching.

A distant metallic clatter echoes from deeper in the facility. A loose panel? A shifting pipe? Whatever it is, it’s enough to make the creature hesitate. It tilts its head, listening.

That’s your chance.

You press forward, step by step, slipping through the dimly lit passageway. The flickering lights ahead reveal something—an intersection. A way forward, but also a choice.

Then—a deep, unnatural growl.

It knows.

You don’t look back. You can’t. You reach the intersection, heart pounding, and make a split-second decision.

Choice 1: Head toward the maintenance tunnels – Cramped, dark, and unstable, but you might be able to lose it.

Choice 2: Go deeper into the facility – Risky, but there could be something useful ahead—a weapon, a terminal, a way out.
