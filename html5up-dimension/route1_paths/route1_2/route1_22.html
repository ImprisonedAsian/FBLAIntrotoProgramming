Silent Steps, Deadly Shadows

You shift ever so slightly, pressing your body lower to the ground, making yourself as small as possible. Your muscles tremble, burning with the effort, but you don’t dare make a sound.

The creature clicks. A slow, deliberate sound, like wet bone scraping against metal. It’s listening. Waiting.

You move again—slow, calculated. Just a few inches, barely breathing as you press deeper into the shadows.

The creature stops.

Did it hear you?

Your heartbeat pounds in your ears. The silence stretches, thick and suffocating. Then—it moves away.

Not far. Just a few steps. But enough.

This is your chance.

Choice 1: Make a break for it – The momentary distraction might be enough to escape.

Choice 2: Keep moving carefully – Slow and silent might be the only way to avoid being caught.
