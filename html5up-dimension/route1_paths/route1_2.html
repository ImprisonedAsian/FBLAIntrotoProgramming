Don't Breathe

You hold your breath and dive into the nearest hiding spot—a narrow maintenance alcove barely wide enough to squeeze into. The cold metal presses against your back as you wedge yourself in, forcing yourself to stay completely still.

The lights flicker. The thing hunting you moves into the corridor.

Its footsteps are slow at first, deliberate. Click. Drag. Click. Drag. The sound of claws scraping against metal sends shivers down your spine.

Then, it stops.

Right outside your hiding spot.

Your heartbeat pounds in your ears. It knows something is here. It just doesn’t know where.

A faint mechanical whirring fills the silence—sensors activating, scanning. The creature lets out a low, guttural growl, then takes one step closer.

You have a choice.

Choice 1: Stay completely still and silent. Maybe it’ll move on.

Choice 2: Try to slowly reposition yourself. Your current spot might not be as hidden as you think.

Choice 3: Hold your breath and prepare to run. If it finds you, you’ll need to act fast.

The creature exhales, a wet, rattling sound. It’s right there.

Decide.
