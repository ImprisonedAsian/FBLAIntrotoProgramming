The Final Mistake

Your instincts scream at you to run, but your body refuses. You freeze.

Heart hammering, you dart into a small maintenance alcove, pressing yourself into the shadows. You hold your breath as the creature’s dragging footsteps grow closer.

The air is thick with rot.

A guttural wheeze escapes the thing’s throat. It knows something is here.

Your fingers tighten against the cold metal wall. If you stay perfectly still, maybe—just maybe—it will pass.

The footsteps pause. The silence stretches. Then, a sharp, wet sniffing sound fills the corridor.

It’s hunting you.

A single word crackles over the intercom. “Error.”

Then, in an instant—pain.

The world flips as something cold and jagged yanks you from the shadows. Clawed, deformed fingers clamp around your arm, twisting unnaturally. You barely manage a strangled gasp before you’re hurled onto the floor.

The last thing you see is a pair of hollow, milky-white eyes staring down at you.

A distorted voice—your own voice—whispers through the darkness:

"You should have run."

Then, the lights flicker once… and go out.

BAD END
