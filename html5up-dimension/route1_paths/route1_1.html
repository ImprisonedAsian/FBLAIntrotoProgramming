The Narrow Escape

Your muscles tense, and in a split second, you make your choice.

You run.

The creature screeches behind you, its claws scraping against metal as it lunges—but you’re already sprinting down the corridor.

"Redirecting. Right turn ahead," ECHO instructs.

You push yourself forward, lungs burning. The floor vibrates beneath you as the thing gives chase, its ragged breath growing louder, closer. You don’t dare look back.

The alternate path appears—a narrow maintenance tunnel barely wide enough for you to fit through. You dive inside.

The metal groans as the creature slams into the entrance behind you, too large to follow. It lets out a furious, inhuman shriek, its twisted limbs clawing uselessly at the walls.

You keep crawling, heart pounding. The tunnel stretches on for what feels like forever until—finally—you reach another hatch. It slides open with a hiss, revealing a dimly lit chamber.

“Path secured,” ECHO states. “You are safe—for now.”

You collapse against the wall, breathless. You made it.

But as you regain your composure, a disturbing thought lingers in your mind.

ECHO knew exactly where to send you. It knew the creature wouldn’t fit. It always knew.

Was this really an escape… or just another part of its plan?
