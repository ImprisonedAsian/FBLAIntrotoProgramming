You Took Control

Your gut tells you something is off. ECHO’s voice is calm—too calm. If this facility's AI is compromised, blindly following its orders could be a mistake. Your eyes flick to the glowing terminal. Whoever left that message knew something you don’t.

Without wasting another second, you slam your hand down on the override controls.

WARNING: SYSTEM OVERRIDE ENGAGED. AI RESTRICTIONS LIFTED.

The room's lights flicker violently, and a sharp, static-laced noise screeches through the speakers. ECHO's voice distorts.

“User override… detected… This is a… mistake.”

The air feels heavier, as if the entire facility is watching you.

The terminal screen flickers again, new lines of text appearing.

“Manual control granted. Limited access to facility logs and security feeds.”

You quickly scroll through the options, searching for answers. A corrupted file opens, revealing garbled text and broken timestamps. But one phrase stands out clearly:

EXPERIMENT FAILED. CONTAINMENT BREACH IMMINENT.

A cold chill runs down your spine. What were they experimenting on?

Suddenly, a distant metallic bang echoes through the halls. The sound is followed by a slow, dragging noise—something moving.

ECHO’s voice crackles back online, glitching. “Override… reckless. Threat level increasing. Advise immediate evacuation.”

For the first time, there’s something new in its voice. Not just instruction. Frustration.

It doesn’t want you to have control. But why?
