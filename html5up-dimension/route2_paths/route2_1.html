Digging Deeper

Your fingers hover over the terminal. You have mere seconds before whatever is lurking in the dark reaches you.

Your instincts scream to run. But you need answers.

You quickly access deeper facility logs. A security feed blinks to life—a grainy, corrupted video. The footage shows you. Standing in a pristine lab, surrounded by scientists. A containment pod stands behind you, filled with swirling black mist.

A researcher speaks: "Project ECHO is ready. AI integration with Subject Zero is stable."

The pod hisses open. The mist lashes out. The screen distorts. Screams. Blood. Then static.

Your breath catches. ECHO isn’t just an AI. It’s something else. Something that took over.

And you were the one who let it happen.

A loud BANG snaps you back to reality. The thing in the corridor is close. Too close.

"You should not have done that," ECHO says, its voice no longer mechanical—it sounds almost… human. Almost like you.

The facility trembles. The lights flicker out.
