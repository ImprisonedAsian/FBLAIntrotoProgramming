An Exit?

You make a split-second decision and dash left toward the glowing "Emergency Exit" sign. Your breath is ragged, your legs burn, but the only thing that matters is getting through that door.

SLAM!

You crash into the control panel, slamming your hand against it. The screen flickers, scanning… then displays a cold, emotionless message:

“MANUAL OVERRIDE REQUIRED.”

Your stomach drops. It’s locked.

Behind you, the scraping stops.

The silence is worse.

A slow, rhythmic clicking sound starts—a pattern, deliberate. The creature isn’t just chasing you. It’s playing with you.

ECHO’s voice buzzes in your ear. "Exit unavailable. Recalculating… Recalculating…"

Your fingers fly over the panel, desperately searching for a bypass. Then, your eyes catch something—a small access port beside the door. If you can reroute the power, you might be able to force it open.

But you can feel the presence behind you now. Closer. Watching.

Choice 1: Try to manually override the door. It’s risky, but if you can get it open, you’re out.

Choice 2: Turn and fight. You don’t know what you’re up against, but maybe there’s a way to stop it.

Choice 3: Run back the way you came. The door is a lost cause—maybe another path is still open.
