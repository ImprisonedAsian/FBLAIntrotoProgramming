Dead End, New Light

You throw yourself through the reinforced doors, forcing them shut behind you. Your fingers scramble over the panel, pressing the lock sequence. A heavy clank echoes as the doors seal.

Your chest heaves as you step back, trying to process your surroundings.

A dead end.

The room is some kind of old security chamber—dust-covered consoles, shattered monitors, and rusting lockers line the walls. The air is thick, stale, carrying the scent of something long forgotten.

Then—the first impact.

A shudder ripples through the doors as the creature slams into them. Metal groans under the force. You spin, frantically searching the room. There has to be something—anything.

Your eyes land on an old, flickering terminal in the corner. The screen is cracked, barely functional, but something faintly glows beneath the dust. You wipe it away with a trembling hand.

A string of numbers blinks weakly on the screen:

4 – 9 – 2

What does it mean?

Before you can think—another crash.

The doors bend inward, deep gashes splitting through the steel. A high-pitched screech pierces the air.

You’re running out of time.

You spin back to the screen, looking for more. But the display glitches, the numbers distorting. Then—the power cuts out entirely.

The room plunges into darkness.

For a second, silence.

Then—the doors burst open.

The creature spills inside, its twisted form silhouetted in the dim emergency glow. Its hollow, gaping maw twitches as it locks onto you.

You stumble back, pressing against the cold wall. Nowhere left to run.

The last thing you see is its shadow engulfing you.

Bad End
