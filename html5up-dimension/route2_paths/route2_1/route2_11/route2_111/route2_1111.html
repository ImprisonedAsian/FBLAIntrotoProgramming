Veil of Steel

The security door hisses open, its reinforced mechanisms groaning as they slide apart. You don’t hesitate—you dash inside, heart hammering, and slam the emergency lock behind you.

A sharp beep.

"Manual lock engaged."

The door seals shut, just as a thundering impact rattles the frame. You stumble back, breath caught in your throat. The creature is here.

Through the reinforced glass panel, you see it—twisted limbs clawing at the metal, its featureless face pressed against the window. It doesn’t screech. It doesn’t roar. It just… stares.

The room is pitch-dark, save for the emergency lights flickering along the floor. The air is stale, heavy with dust and disuse.

Then you notice it—this isn’t just a security checkpoint. It’s a control hub.

Rows of inactive monitors line the walls, some shattered, others frozen on distorted images of the facility. A large central terminal hums softly, a faint green glow pulsating on its screen.

And on the far side of the room—another door.

This one is older, rust creeping along its hinges. A faded warning label is barely visible beneath the grime:

"AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY – FACILITY SYSTEMS CONTROL."

You approach the central console, wiping dust from the interface. A prompt blinks at you:

"SYSTEM ACCESS RESTRICTED. AUTHORIZATION REQUIRED."

"ENTER CLEARANCE CODE OR INITIATE FAILSAFE PROTOCOL."

Your pulse quickens.

This could be it—a way to shut the creature out for good. Maybe even access something deeper in the facility… if you have the right credentials.

But you don’t have the clearance code.

Behind you, the creature claws at the glass, its inhuman patience unsettling.

You need to act. Fast.

Choice 1: Attempt a manual bypass on the system – Risk tripping security protocols, but you might unlock something crucial.

Choice 2: Forget the console and push forward through the rusted door – It might lead deeper into the facility… or a dead end.
